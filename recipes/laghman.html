Laghman

Laghman is a dish of meat, vegetables and pulled noodles from Uyghur cuisine and Central Asian cuisine.

Lagman is prepared with meat (mainly lamb or beef),vegetables and pulled long noodles. The vegetables usually include Bulgarian peppers, eggplants, radish, potatoes, onions, garlic, and spices.

Ingridiends:

For the Noodles

  • 2 cups of flour,
  • 3/4 cup of water at room temperature,
  • 1 tablespoon of salt,
  • Sunflower oil.

For the meat Sauce

  • 500 g lamb meat, chopped,
  • 1 onion, chopped,
  • 1 red pepper, chopped coarsely,
  • 2 green peppers, chopped coarsely,
  • 2 cloves of garlic,
  • 1 chili pepper or 1 tablespoon of hot pepper sauce,
  • 2 tablespoons of balsamic vinegar (Chinese black vinegar),
  • 1 cup of boiling water,
  • Salt,
  • Pepper.

Steps:

  1. For the meat sauce, heat a large pan on the stove,
  2. Add the meat and stir fry on high heat until it releases its juice,
  3. Add the oil and chopped onion and stir fry until the onions soften,
  4. Add the coarsely chopped peppers and mix,
  5. Cook for a few minutes, stirring constantly,
  6. Add sliced ​​garlic, thinly sliced ​​chili or hot pepper sauce, balsamic vinegar, salt, pepper and water and mix,
  7. Close the lid and cook until the water thickens and then turn off the heat,
  8. For the noodles, mix the flour and salt in a bowl,
  9. Add water little by little and knead until you get a non-sticky dough,
  10. Take it on the counter and knead for 5 minutes,
  11. Put it back in the bowl, cover it and let it rest for 15 minutes,
  12. Flatten the dough with your hands and cut it into 6 thick strips,
  13. Press the strips with both hands on the counter and turn them into pinky finger-thick wicks/cylinders with back and forth movements,
  14. Cover the wicks with oil and place them on an oiled tray, starting from the middle and wrapping them around,
  15. Cover it with cling film and let it rest for 15 minutes,
  16. Pull out the dough pieces and thin them,
  17. Wrap them between your two hands and hit them on the counter,
  18. Boil plenty of water in a large pot,
  19. Place the noodles in the boiling water and stir immediately so that they do not stick,
  20. Once it boils, add 1/4 cup of room temperature water to the pot,
  21. When it boils again, add 1/4 cup of room temperature water again,
  22. When it boils again, turn off the heat,
  23. Drain the water and rinse with running water,
  24. Take the noodles on serving plates and share the heated meat sauce over or mix them with meat sauce and serve.
